Mason's WEB110 Portal page

I hold degrees in Mathematics and Computer Science from Colorado State University. I specialized in number theory, cryptography, and parallel programming. I also have a minor in Physics, and have taken the Game Development certification from University of Washington Extension, which has given me a working knowledge of real-time 3d graphics in DirectX.

My first job out of school was a Software Development Engineer (SDET) with Microsoft. It was an excellent education for software engineering in a large corporate environment. I worked on a variety of products over that time: Windows XP, Vista, Internet Explorer, and Flight Simulator. In the SDET capacity I found myself focusing too narrowly on the technologies and not broadening my technical skills. So I am great with C++ and C#, and not so great in the internet space. Go figure, a Microsoft employee who is not good at the internets. My goal is to work independantly, or at the least for an independant company. I am also eager to grow my skills and background in open source technologies to balance my knowledge of Microsoft's products.

My long term goals are to work further in video games or mathematics. I put my college career on hold to work for Microsoft. Overall I think it was good, but I often wonder what would've come from a graduate degree in mathematics. I love to teach and would like to think I will find my way back there eventually. I am currently investigating jobs in the finance industry as an SDET, and in the internet or gaming industries as a developer.

My other hobbies include music; I am not much of a guitar player, but love seeking out new music. I also am interested in sound engineering, but think I am more interested in the programmatic aspects than just using ProTools or similar. Of course I enjoy playing video games, and am trying to improve my animation and design skills. I also love to read strange fiction, horror, and science fiction. My favorite thing to do when travelling is seeking out used book stores and rifling through their selection. I am also writing a novel in the cyberpunk genre. I like to think that all of my hobbies could be integrated into a one-man game studio. Heh.
